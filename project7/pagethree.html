Tips and Tricks to Home Cooking

Cooking in college or on your own can become stressful quickly if you are not prepared. On this page, I will be sharing some information on how I try to manage cooking for myself while also balancing college classes, a job, and a social life. Some topics I will be going over are how to plan, budget, and shop so that you can make the most of the process.

Home cooking offers so many benefits and it is a very important life skill to learn how to cook for yourself and others so that you can be self-sufficient. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced cook, hopefully, these tips can help you make the most of your solo cooking adventures!

Additionally, as a college student, I understand the challenges of navigating a tight schedule. In the midst of assignments and deadlines, finding time to cook nutritious meals is crucial. I'll be sharing insights on quick and healthy recipes that fit into a busy lifestyle. From time-saving techniques to meal prepping strategies, my goal is to help you enjoy the process of cooking even when time is limited.

Plan Your Meals

To prepare each week you can create a meal plan for what you will be eating this way you can avoid having to make last-minute decisions. You can also try to prepare your meals in advance to save time when you know you have a busy day coming up. Planning your meals can also include trying to decide which ingredients you can get that can be used in a variety of ways for less food waste. Planning is a key step in the cooking process and can be done in a variety of different ways. My personal favorite method is to keep a notes app going on my phone so that way throughout the week when I think about ingredients or meals, I want to make sure I write them there, so I remember when I’m going shopping. This helps keep me organized the best, but you could also use many other methods.

Invest In Quality Ingredients

Investing in quality ingredients can be especially challenging if you are a college student working around a budget, but it is also an important step when home cooking. When you use fresh, high-quality ingredients it leads to a better flavor and is more nutritious. My favorite place to shop for cheaper but still high-quality ingredients would have to be Trader Joe’s, they have the best prices for great quality products which is very hard to find these days. Another trick to investing in high-quality ingredients can be to buy in bulk when possible. This is important because it can help you save money in the long run for better products.

Portion Control

Portion control is an essential step in home cooking especially if you are only cooking for yourself. You should pay attention to the portion sizes you are giving yourself. This is helpful because it lets you avoid overeating while also helping to not waste food. Some tricks for this could be learning how to cut recipes in ½ or ¼ if necessary so that you aren’t making a meal for 8 for just yourself. It is also helpful to know what meals stay good in the fridge or freezer so that way if you do have leftovers, you can save them for another time. If you want to take it a step further, you could even invest in a kitchen scale. This way you know you are measuring your ingredients correctly.

Stock Your Pantry

It is inevitable that there will come a time when you need something quick and easy for whatever reason, therefore it is important to make sure you are keeping your pantry stocked. Some of my favorite essential items to keep stocked up are rice, pasta, and canned goods like soup. These are important to have because they are still nutritious options, but they are also quicker to make on nights when you are feeling too tired to cook.

Start With Simple Recipies

When you are first starting your cooking journey you should take it slow. It is important to not jump right into complex meals and start out with more simple options. You can begin with easy-to-follow recipes and then gradually challenge yourself with more complex dishes. You can also practice mastering some of the basic cooking techniques like sauteing, roasting, and boiling which are used in a variety of different dishes.

Enjoy The Process

Finally, I want to note that the main thing you should be worried about is just enjoying the process of cooking and having fun with it. Cooking by yourself can be a pleasurable and meditative experience when you want it to be. You should savor the meals that you cook and be proud of yourself for even preparing them in the first place!
